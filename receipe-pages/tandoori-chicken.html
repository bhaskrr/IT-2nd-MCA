Tandoori Chicken

Tandoori Chicken is a classic Indian dish that captivates with its vibrant flavors and enticing aroma. Tender chicken pieces are marinated in a blend of yogurt and spices like ginger, garlic, cumin, and coriander. The marinated chicken is then traditionally cooked in a tandoor oven, resulting in a smoky charred exterior and succulent, juicy meat. The bright red hue of the chicken comes from the use of spices like paprika or Kashmiri red chili powder. Tandoori Chicken is known for its tantalizing blend of tanginess, spiciness, and mild smokiness. Served with mint chutney and lemon wedges, it's a true delight for all chicken lovers, offering a flavorful journey into the heart of Indian cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • - 1.5 kg chicken pieces (drumsticks, thighs, or bone-in chicken)
  • - 1 cup plain yogurt
  • - 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • - 2 tablespoons tandoori masala (readily available or make your own with a blend of spices like paprika, cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, and cloves)
  • - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • - 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • - 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • - 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • - 2 tablespoons oil
  • - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • - 2 cups water
  • - Salt to taste
  • - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • - Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, tandoori masala, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, oil, and salt to form a smooth marinade.
  2. Make deep slits on the chicken pieces to allow the marinade to penetrate and coat the meat thoroughly.
  3. Rub the chicken pieces with the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover the bowl with cling film and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results.
  4. Preheat the oven to 220째C (425째F). If using a grill, preheat it to medium-high heat.
  5. Place the marinated chicken pieces on a baking tray lined with foil or directly on the grill. Reserve any leftover marinade for basting.
  6. Bake or grill the chicken for about 20-25 minutes, flipping once or twice, until it is cooked through and has a slight char on the edges. The chicken should have an internal temperature of 165째F (75째C) when fully cooked.
  7. While the chicken is cooking, baste it with the leftover marinade every 5-10 minutes to keep it moist and enhance the flavors.
  8. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the oven or grill. Let it rest for a few minutes.
  9. Garnish the Tandoori Chicken with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.

Tandoori Chicken is often served as a standalone appetizer or as part of a main course. It pairs well with mint chutney, naan bread, or rice. Enjoy preparing and sharing this delicious Tandoori Chicken recipe!
