Litti Chokha

Litti Chokha is a rustic and flavorful dish from Bihar, India. It consists of roasted wheat flour dumplings (litti) stuffed with a spicy mashed vegetable mixture (chokha). The litti is made from a dough of whole wheat flour and gram flour, while the chokha is a blend of mashed potatoes, tomatoes, onions, green chilies, and spices. The litti is traditionally roasted over charcoal or baked until golden brown. The dish is served with ghee or melted butter, along with pickles and yogurt. Litti Chokha is loved for its smoky flavors, spicy chokha filling, and the perfect balance of textures. It is a favorite street food and a must-try for those seeking an authentic taste of Bihar.

Ingredients:

For Litti:

  • - 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • - 1/2 cup gram flour (besan)
  • - 1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • - 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • - Salt to taste
  • - Water for kneading

For Chokha:

  • - 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • - 1 large tomato, finely chopped
  • - 1 onion, finely chopped
  • - 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • - 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • - 1 tablespoon mustard oil
  • - Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, gram flour, carom seeds, ghee or oil, and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a firm dough. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, prepare the chokha. In a bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, chopped tomato, onion, green chilies, coriander leaves, mustard oil, lemon juice, and salt. Mix well to combine all the ingredients. Adjust the seasoning according to taste.
  3. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F). Alternatively, you can cook the litti on a charcoal grill or in a tandoor oven.
  4. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized portions. Roll each portion into a round ball and flatten it slightly with your palms.
  5. Stuff each flattened dough ball with a spoonful of the prepared chokha mixture. Seal the edges and reshape it into a smooth ball.
  6. Place the stuffed litti on a baking tray lined with parchment paper or directly on a greased grill rack. Bake or grill the litti for about 15-20 minutes, flipping once or twice, until they turn golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Once cooked, remove the litti from the oven or grill. Brush them with a little ghee for added flavor.
  8. Serve the hot litti with a side of chokha. It is traditionally enjoyed with ghee, pickle, and yogurt.
