Dal Makhani

Dal Makhani is a classic North Indian dish that embodies comfort and richness. It is a creamy and buttery lentil preparation made with black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma), slow-cooked with aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The lentils are simmered for hours until they become soft and creamy, and the flavors meld together. The addition of cream and butter towards the end gives it a luscious and velvety texture. Dal Makhani is often enjoyed with naan or rice, and its indulgent taste makes it a favorite at special occasions and restaurants.

Ingredients:

  • - 1 cup whole black lentils (sabut urad dal), soaked overnight and drained
  • - 1/4 cup kidney beans (rajma), soaked overnight and drained
  • - 2 tablespoons butter
  • - 1 tablespoon oil
  • - 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • - 2 tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • - 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • - 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • - 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • - 1/2 cup cream or full-fat yogurt
  • - Salt to taste
  • - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pressure cooker or large pot, add the soaked and drained lentils and kidney beans. Add enough water to cover them by about an inch. Cook until they are tender and fully cooked (around 4-5 whistles in a pressure cooker).
  2. Heat butter and oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add pureed or chopped tomatoes, green chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and the spices are well combined.
  6. Add the cooked lentils and kidney beans to the pan, along with any remaining cooking water. Mix well and bring it to a simmer. Cook for about 15-20 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld together.
  7. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the pan. Mix well.
  8. Add cream or yogurt to the pan and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Garnish the Dal Makhani with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with naan, roti or rice.
