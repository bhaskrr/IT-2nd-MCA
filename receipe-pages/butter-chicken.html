Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a popular Indian dish that has gained international recognition. It is a creamy and flavorful chicken curry that features tender chicken pieces cooked in a rich tomato-based gravy.

Ingredients:

  • - 500 grams boneless chicken, cut into pieces
  • - 2 tablespoons butter
  • - 1 tablespoon oil
  • - 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • - 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • - 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • - 1/4 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water
  • - 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • - 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • - 1 teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi)
  • - 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • - Salt to taste
  • - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat butter and oil in a pan over medium heat. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste to the pan and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add tomato puree to the pan and cook for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oil separates from the mixture.
  4. Meanwhile, drain the soaked cashew nuts and grind them into a smooth paste using a blender or food processor.
  5. Add the cashew paste to the pan and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Stir to combine the spices with the gravy.
  7. Add about 1/2 cup of water to adjust the consistency of the gravy. Simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  8. In a separate pan, heat a little butter or oil and lightly sauté the chicken pieces until they are browned. Add the cooked chicken to the tomato gravy and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  9. Crush the dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) between your palms and sprinkle them into the gravy. Stir well.
  10. Stir in the heavy cream and simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste.
  11. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
`

Serve the Butter Chicken hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice. It pairs well with a side of raita (yogurt dip) and salad. The creamy and flavorful tomato gravy makes this dish a favorite among chicken lovers.
