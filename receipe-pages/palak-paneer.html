Palak Paneer

Palak Paneer is a popular vegetarian dish from North Indian cuisine. It consists of creamy spinach (palak) gravy cooked with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes. This dish is not only delicious but also nutritious due to the high iron content in spinach.

Ingredients:

  • - 200 grams paneer, cubed
  • - 500 grams spinach leaves
  • - 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • - 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • - 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • - 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • - 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • - 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • - 2 tablespoons ghee or oil/li>
  • - 2 tablespoons cream (optional)
  • - Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Clean the spinach leaves thoroughly, removing any stems or tough parts. Blanch the spinach leaves in boiling water for 2-3 minutes until wilted. Drain the spinach and transfer it to a bowl of ice water to retain its green color. Drain again and blend the spinach into a smooth puree. Set aside.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Then add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste to the pan and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and mushy.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a minute to let the spices release their flavors.
  6. Add the spinach puree to the pan and stir well. Cook for 4-5 minutes on medium heat to let the flavors blend together.
  7. Add salt to taste and adjust the consistency of the gravy by adding water if needed. Simmer for a few minutes.
  8. Add paneer cubes to the spinach gravy and mix gently. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the paneer is heated through.
  9. Optional: Stir in cream to add richness to the gravy. Simmer for a minute and remove from heat.
  10. Serve the Palak Paneer hot with naan, roti, or rice.
`

Palak Paneer is a wholesome and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed by vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike. Its vibrant green color and creamy texture make it visually appealing. You can garnish it with a drizzle of cream and serve it with a side of raita (yogurt dip) or salad to complete the meal.
