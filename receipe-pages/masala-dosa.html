Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a beloved South Indian dish that has gained popularity worldwide. It is a crispy and savory crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, typically filled with a spiced potato filling. Served with coconut chutney and sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew), Masala Dosa offers a delightful combination of textures and flavors. The crispy dosa provides a satisfying crunch, while the spicy and aromatic potato filling adds depth and richness to each bite. The accompanying chutneys and sambar complement the dosa perfectly, adding a tangy and savory element to the overall experience.

Ingredients:

For Dosa Batter:

  • - 1 cup parboiled rice (ukda chawal)
  • - 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • - 1/4 cup flattened rice (poha)
  • - 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • - Water for soaking and grinding
  • - Salt to taste

For Potato Masala Filling:

  1. 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  2. 1 onion, finely chopped
  3. 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  4. 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  5. 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  6. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  7. A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  8. A few curry leaves
  9. 1 tablespoon oil
  10. Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large pan or kadai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes turn soft and mushy.
  5. Reduce the heat to low. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a minute.
  6. Add the cauliflower florets and potato cubes to the pan. Stir gently to coat the vegetables with the spices.
  7. Cover the pan with a lid and cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until the cauliflower and potatoes are tender, stirring occasionally. If needed, sprinkle a little water to prevent sticking.
  8. Sprinkle garam masala over the cooked vegetables and mix well. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  9. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.

Masala Dosa is a flavorful and satisfying South Indian dish that is enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. The crispy dosa filled with a spiced potato filling is a delightful combination. Enjoy the authentic flavors of South India with this delicious Masala Dosa recipe!
