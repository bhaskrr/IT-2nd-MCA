Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a beloved North Indian dish that consists of spicy chickpea curry (chole) served with deep-fried bread (bhature). The chickpeas are cooked in a flavorful blend of onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The curry is rich, tangy, and has a slight heat, making it a perfect companion to the fluffy and crispy bhature. The bhature is made from a fermented dough of all-purpose flour, yogurt, and a pinch of baking powder. It is then rolled out into a round shape and deep-fried until it puffs up and turns golden brown. Chole Bhature is often served with pickles, yogurt, and a side of sliced onions.

Ingredients:

For Chole(Chickpea Curry):

  • - 1 cup dried chickpeas (kabuli chana), soaked overnight and drained
  • - 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • - 2 medium-sized tomatoes, finely chopped
  • - 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • - 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • - 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust according to spice preference)
  • - 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • - 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • - 1 tablespoon oil
  • - Salt to taste
  • - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

For Bhature(Fried Bread):

  • - 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • - 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • - 1 teaspoon sugar
  • - 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • - 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • - 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • - Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

For Chole(Chickpea Curry)

  1. In a pressure cooker or large pot, add soaked and drained chickpeas along with enough water to cover them. Cook until the chickpeas are tender (around 4-5 whistles in a pressure cooker).
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and the spices are well combined.
  6. Add the cooked chickpeas (along with the water) to the pan. Mix well and bring it to a simmer. Cook for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala over the chole and mix well. Cook for an additional 2 minutes. Adjust the seasoning if needed.
  8. Garnish the Chole with fresh coriander leaves.

For Bhature(Fried Bread)

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, plain yogurt, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Add water gradually and knead the dough until it is smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 1-2 hours.
  3. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized portions. Roll each portion into a round shape, about 1/4 inch thick.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium-high heat for deep frying.
  5. Carefully slide the rolled dough into the hot oil and fry until it puffs up and turns golden brown on both sides. Flip it once to ensure even cooking.
  6. Remove the fried bhatura from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain the excess oil on a paper towel.
  7. Serve the hot and fluffy Bhature with the prepared Chole. It is traditionally enjoyed with pickles, sliced onions, and a side of yogurt.

Chole Bhature is a delightful and satisfying dish that brings together the flavors of spicy chickpea curry and fluffy fried bread. Enjoy this classic North Indian delicacy with your family and friends!
