Jadoh

Jadoh is a traditional rice dish from the northeastern state of Meghalaya, India. It is a flavorful and hearty preparation that combines rice with meat, usually pork, along with a blend of aromatic spices. The rice is cooked with the meat, onions, ginger, garlic, and a variety of local spices, resulting in a dish that is rich in flavors and textures. The meat imparts its savory essence to the rice, creating a delightful combination. Jadoh is known for its comforting taste and is often enjoyed as a main course or during festive occasions.

Ingredients:

  • - 1 cup basmati rice
  • - 250 grams boneless chicken or pork, cut into small pieces (you can also use smoked pork for an authentic flavor)
  • - 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • - 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • - 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • - 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • - 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • - Salt to taste
  • - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • - Fresh coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan or pressure cooker over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn translucent.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies to the pan. Cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the chicken or pork pieces to the pan and cook until they are lightly browned.
  5. Stir in the turmeric powder, ground black pepper, and salt. Mix well to coat the meat with the spices.
  6. Add the soaked basmati rice to the pan. Stir gently to mix the rice with the meat and spices.
  7. Pour in enough water to cook the rice (the ratio of rice to water is usually 1:2). Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  8. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed. If using a pressure cooker, cook for about 2-3 whistles.
  9. Once cooked, fluff the rice gently with a fork. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves if desired.
  10. Serve hot Jadoh as a main course or as a part of a meal. It pairs well with a side of raita (yogurt-based side dish) or pickle.

Jadoh is a comforting and flavorful rice dish that highlights the culinary traditions of Meghalaya. The combination of tender meat, aromatic spices, and fragrant basmati rice creates a delicious and satisfying meal. Enjoy the flavors of Meghalaya with this authentic Jadoh recipe!
